###  Spicy Garlic Fish Fry / Garlic pepper Fish Fry / பூண்டு மிளகு மீன் வறுவல்

Try this garlic flavored, spicy fish fry as a starter / main course; which I
learnt from my friend Preethi Jothivel.  
  
Though I used to prepare fish fry often; this base ingredient is different
with all flavorful ingredients like garlic, pepper & fennel!.  So I want to
give it a try!  Yummy!  
  
Thank you Preethi for sharing the recipe!  
  
Let's check the recipe now,  

  

PREP TIME

15 min

COOK TIME

5  mins

TOTAL TIME

20 mins

                                                                                                                                                   

Recipe for Garlic Fish Fry

Recipe type: Main course

Cuisine:  South Indian

Serves: 4  to 6 (approximately)

**_  
_****_Ingredients_ :**  
Fish - 2 to 3  
Salt - to taste  

Curry leaves - 1 sprig  
Coconut oil / Vegetable oil - 2 to 3 tbsp  
 **To grind:**  
Garlic - 6 to 8  
Pepper - 2 tsp  
Fennel seeds - 1 tsp  
Turmeric powder - ½ tsp  
Red chilli powder - 1 tsp  
Tamarind paste / lemon juice - 2 tsp

  
  

****  
**_Directions_ :**  
Wash and clean the fish pieces; make incisions and pat dry.  
  
Grind together garlic, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, fennel seeds,
pepper, tamarind extract / lemon juice, salt and just enough water to make
fine thick paste.  

Apply this masala on both sides of the fish; allow to marinate for more than a
hour.

  
Before frying, just add little corn flour over fish and apply it.  
  
Heat oil in a flat bottom pan, add some curry leaves and place the fish.

  

Turn the fish in between to cook both sides evenly.(Do this in a medium flame)  
  
Serve hot as a starter or accompaniment to rice with fish curry.  

  

**Note:**

This recipe is suitable for any kind of fish.  
  
You can adjust the amount of pepper and red chilli powder according to your
taste.  
  
Adding curry leaves is optional but I preferred to add because it gives a nice
flavor to the fish  
  
Serve hot as a starter or accompaniment to rice with fish curry (Meen
kulambu); even with rasam it is delicious!
